The Film

On a hot summer day in Brooklyn, Derrick, a smart ass, punk rock-infatuated thirteen-year-old, steals ice cream from an ice cream truck belonging to his neighbour, Neesh, a grumpy but loveable musicologist. As a punishment, Neesh makes Derrick “work off his debt” by having Derrick help sell ice cream. After spending most of the day arguing with each other, Neesh and Derrick manage to meet in the middle, bonding over a common love for music.

Ice Cream Men is a film that represents my culture and beliefs as closely as I can imagine. It holds themes central to my heart involving my love for music, my Indian heritage, and, of course, ice cream.

The Budget

Above is the total budget breakdown for the film. Your contributions will be helping the ice cream truck, food and lodging.

In addition, permits granted by the Mayor’s Office of New York City are required, as we are filming in public locations with a moving vehicle. These permits allow us to close down streets and therefore ensure safety for the cast, crew and pedestrians.

With such specific cultural references, the art department will require a sizeable amount to make the truck feel vibrant and unique to express Neesh’s Indian heritage and his rock n’ roll roots.

Finally, copyright music is notoriously expensive but is essential for this film. Without it, an entire dimension of who these characters are will be lost.
